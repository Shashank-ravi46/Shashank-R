Project 01

Pillar 3 Capital & Liquidity Analysis Pack

A multi-bank regulatory analysis pack built to track capital strength, liquidity resilience, and credit-risk indicators across major UK banks using public disclosures.

Tools used Excel, annual reports, Pillar 3 disclosures, ratio analysis, data validation logic
Dashboard / output Standardised monitoring pack with trend views, metric checks, commentary blocks, and analyst-style summaries

Business problem

Public bank disclosures are dense, inconsistent, and difficult to compare quickly. The project solves that by converting fragmented capital and liquidity data into one comparable analyst pack across four UK banks.

What I did

  • Standardised raw Pillar 3 and annual report data into a clean comparison structure.
  • Calculated CET1, total capital, leverage ratio, LCR, NSFR, and selected credit-risk metrics.
  • Embedded checks to validate consistency, reduce input risk, and improve trust in outputs.
  • Built trend analysis and commentary sections to explain movement over time, not just display figures.

Key insights

The pack highlights how each bank’s capital and liquidity profile evolved over time, where resilience strengthened, and where risk trends or balance sheet pressures became more visible. It also demonstrates the trade-off between headline ratios and underlying risk quality.

Impact

Converted a regulatory reporting problem into a recruiter-ready portfolio asset that shows practical banking analysis, structured Excel modelling, and clear financial commentary. The result is a stronger proof point for FP&A, banking analytics, and regulatory reporting applications.

Project 02

Equity Valuation & Portfolio Decision Framework

A repeatable stock analysis framework used to assess listed companies, compare valuation scenarios, and support evidence-based portfolio allocation decisions.

Tools used Excel, DCF, comparable company analysis, ratio analysis, sector research
Dashboard / output Investment case templates, scenario tabs, valuation range outputs, and thesis-versus-outcome review pages

Business problem

Investment decisions break down when analysis is inconsistent or purely opinion-based. The project creates a disciplined framework for comparing opportunities on the basis of valuation, quality, and risk/reward.

What I did

  • Analysed 20+ listed companies using financial statements, ratios, and sector drivers.
  • Built DCF and comp models with sensitivity analysis to test upside, downside, and assumption risk.
  • Produced written investment cases tied to portfolio allocation discussions.
  • Reviewed portfolio performance against original thesis and market benchmarks.

Key insights

Good analysis is not just about finding undervaluation. It is about understanding where expectations are wrong, what could break the thesis, and how market outcomes compare with the original logic.

Impact

Turned company research into allocation-relevant output, improving the quality of portfolio discussions and demonstrating practical buy-side style analysis rather than classroom-only finance theory.

Project 03

Trading Strategy Performance & Attribution Tracker

A simulation-based performance tracker designed to evaluate strategies through factor logic, exposure monitoring, attribution reporting, and clean execution control.

Tools used Excel, Python, scenario modelling, P&L tracking, benchmark comparison
Dashboard / output Strategy cycle reports, exposure summaries, performance attribution views, and trade control logs

Business problem

Strategy ideas mean very little without disciplined measurement. The tracker was built to evaluate whether returns came from skill, factor exposure, or luck, while maintaining tight process control around execution.

What I did

  • Developed factor- and scenario-based trading models across a 6-month live market simulation.
  • Monitored P&L, exposures, and positions against benchmarks each cycle.
  • Produced attribution reports that explained drivers of gains and losses.
  • Executed trades from instruction to settlement without booking or settlement errors.

Key insights

The process made it easier to separate strategy quality from noise, identify weak assumptions early, and improve reporting discipline under changing market conditions.

Impact

Demonstrated that I can combine analysis with operational discipline, which matters in roles that require both insight generation and precise execution.
